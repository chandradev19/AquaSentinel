## Slide 2: Problem Statement & Existing Flaws
**The Challenge:**
Waterborne diseases (Cholera, Typhoid, Diarrhea) account for significant mortality and economic loss in rural and semi-urban India due to contaminated water sources.

**Existing Problems:**
1. **Reactive, Not Proactive:** Authorities only act *after* a severe outbreak happens and casualties are reported.
2. **Siloed Data:** Water quality boards, health ministries, and local clinics do not share real-time data.
3. **Lack of Predictive Power:** No mechanism to correlate weather anomalies (like monsoon rainfall) and local water quality to predict future outbreaks.
4. **Poor Citizen Engagement:** Citizens lack a direct channel to report symptoms or receive hyper-local warnings.

---

## Slide 3: Our Proposed Solution (AquaShield AI)
A unified, AI-driven Health Intelligence platform that bridges the gap between citizens, field workers, and national authorities.

**Key Innovations:**
1. **AI Disease Prediction:** Utilizes Weka's RandomForest ML algorithms to predict outbreaks before they happen by analyzing pH, turbidity, symptoms, and rainfall.
2. **Village Digital Twin:** A cybernetic replica of every village showing real-time health and risk telemetry.
3. **Automated Risk Engine:** Instantly calculates dynamic risk scores for every district in India.
4. **Emergency Broadcast Network:** Real-time, targeted alerts sent directly to affected citizens and health officers.

## Slide 5: The Citizen & Field Worker Workflow
**Citizen Portal:**

**Field Officer App:**
- Health workers receive dispatch orders.
- Workers input water quality tests directly from the field.
- Verify citizen reports to prevent false alarms.

## Slide 6: The AI Engine & Outbreak Simulator
**How it works:**
1. Collects structured data (verified symptoms, water pH, historical timelines).
2. ML models compute the probability of a specific pathogen outbreak (e.g., V. cholerae).

**Outbreak Simulator Module:**
- Allows authorities to run "What-If" scenarios.
- *Example:* "If rainfall increases by 50mm and contamination hits 40%, what is the epidemic risk?"
- The AI instantly provides mitigation recommendations.
